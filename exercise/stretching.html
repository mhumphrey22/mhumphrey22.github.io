Comprehensive Full-Body Warm-Up Sheet

Purpose: Prepares the body for heavy barbell lifting (e.g., Starting Strength). Combines lower body mobility (Limber 11) with upper body activation and joint prep.

Estimated Total Time: 20–25 minutes

Lower Body (Limber 11 - ~10–15 minutes)

  1. Foam Roll IT Band – 10-15 passes per side
    Description: Lie on your side with the foam roller placed just below your hip and slowly roll down toward your knee, targeting the outer thigh. Support yourself with your upper body and opposite leg to control pressure. While the IT band itself doesn’t stretch easily, this technique helps relieve tension and reduce friction around the hip and knee, especially useful for runners and lifters.
  2. Foam Roll Adductors – 10-15 passes per side
    Description: Position yourself face down with one leg bent out to the side and the foam roller under your inner thigh (adductor area). Roll slowly from the groin to just above the knee. This is great for opening up the inner thighs and improving hip abduction, which supports better squat mechanics.
  3. Foam Roll Glutes – 30s-2m per side
    Description: Sit on the foam roller with one ankle crossed over the opposite knee in a figure-four position. Lean into the glute of the crossed leg and roll slowly. This targets the glute max and piriformis, helping release tension that contributes to back or hip pain and prepares the posterior chain for loading.
  4. Bent-Knee Iron Cross – 5-10 reps per side
    Description: Lie flat on your back with both arms extended at shoulder level. Bend your knees to 90 degrees and slowly rotate your legs side to side, aiming to touch the ground. This controlled twist activates and stretches the lower back and hips, improving rotational mobility in the lumbar spine and thoracic region.
  5. Rollovers to V-Sits – 10 reps
    Description: Begin lying on your back, then roll your legs over your head and transition into a seated V-sit with legs extended. This movement dynamically mobilizes the spine, hamstrings, and hips. It also activates core control and builds fluidity between posterior chain movement patterns.
  6. Rocking Frog Stretch – 10 reps
    Description: Start on all fours with knees wide and feet turned out. Lower your hips back and then rock forward again, keeping your spine neutral. This dynamic movement stretches the groin and adductors while improving hip external rotation—ideal for deep squat prep.
  7. Fire Hydrants – 10 reps per side, forwards and backwards
    Description: In a quadruped (hands and knees) position, lift one knee out to the side while keeping it bent at 90 degrees. Perform circles forward and backward. This strengthens the glute medius and improves hip control, helping stabilize the pelvis during loaded lower-body movements.
  8. Mountain Climbers – 10 reps per side
    Description: From a plank position, alternate driving your knees toward your chest rapidly. This increases heart rate and body temperature, while dynamically activating the hip flexors, core, and shoulders. It’s a powerful metabolic warm-up and great prep for squats or deadlifts.
  9. Cossack Squats – 5-10 reps per side
    Description: Stand with feet in a wide stance. Shift your weight onto one leg, squatting down while keeping the other leg extended. Keep your chest upright and heel down. This stretch improves hip adduction, hamstring flexibility, and lateral mobility — especially useful for athletic movement patterns and deep squatting.
  10. Seated Piriformis Stretch – 20-30s per side
    Description: Sit on the ground with one leg extended and the other crossed over. Hug the bent knee toward your chest, rotating your torso slightly toward it. This deeply stretches the piriformis and glute muscles, reducing hip tightness and alleviating lower back pressure or sciatica symptoms.
  11. Rear-foot-elevated Hip Flexor Stretch – 5x10 reps, with 3s hold, per side
    Description: In a lunge position, place your back foot on a bench or box. Push your hips forward while staying tall and squeezing the glute of the back leg. This intensifies the stretch in the hip flexor and quad of the rear leg — essential for those who sit a lot or struggle to hit full depth in squats.

Upper Body Activation + Mobility (~10 minutes)

  1. Wall Slides – 2x10 reps
    Description: Slide arms upward against a wall while maintaining contact to improve shoulder mobility and posture.
  2. Band Pull-Aparts – 2x15 reps
    Description: Pull a resistance band apart at shoulder height to activate the upper back and scapular stabilizers.
  3. Scapular Wall Angels – 2x10 reps
    Description: Perform arm circles against the wall focusing on scapular retraction and upward rotation.
  4. Banded Shoulder Dislocates – 2x15 reps
    Description: Slowly pass a band over and behind the head to open up the shoulders.
  5. Thoracic Spine Extensions – 1–2 minutes
    Description: Use a foam roller under your upper back to gently extend the thoracic spine.
  6. Doorway Pec Stretch – 30 seconds per side
    Description: Lean into a doorway with your forearm on the frame to stretch your chest and anterior shoulder.
  7. Wrist Extension Stretch – 30 seconds per side
    Description: Extend your arm, palm up, and gently pull your fingers downward to stretch the wrist flexors.

Optional Additions (as needed)

  1. Face Pulls (Band) – 2x15 reps
    Description: Pull a band toward your face at eye level to activate rear delts and improve shoulder posture.
  2. Child’s Pose + Side Reach – 30 seconds per side
    Description: From child’s pose, walk your arms to the side to stretch the lats and thoracic spine.
  3. Ankle Dorsiflexion Stretch – 1–2 mins per ankle
    Description: Drive your knee over your toe while keeping your heel down to improve ankle mobility.

Tip: Adjust volume and intensity based on time, fatigue, and target lift.
